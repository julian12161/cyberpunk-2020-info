    Cousin's office

    The office smells like cold coffee and stale noodles. A half-eaten takeout box sits on the desk, the contents long congealed. The chair’s still slightly turned, like someone stood up mid-bite and never came back.
    The lights are off, but the ambient glow of the city leaks in through the blinds, striping the room in flickering orange and blue. The terminal on the desk is dead, not shut down—just abruptly powered off.
    Paper files are rare, but one still lies open on the desk—a real folder, creased, like it was gripped tightly before being dropped. Inside: a printed copy of a contract between the cousin’s employer and Biotechnica. Supply chains, security protocols, and new patents.
