    Cousin's apartment

    The door slides open with a soft hiss, revealing a small but well-kept space wrapped in quiet stillness. The air is musty, not foul—just the scent of a place that’s gone untouched for a few weeks too long. No flies, no rot. Just dust.
    Everything is neat, like someone meant to come home. The kitchenette is clean—utensils in their holders, dishes washed and stacked, a coffee mug turned upside-down to dry. A coat still hangs on the wall by the door. The sofa cushions are undisturbed, and a folded blanket rests where it always did. Nothing’s been rummaged, nothing rushed.
    No signs of struggle. Just absence.
    The bedroom is equally tidy—bed made, terminal powered off at the desk. A single framed photo sits on the shelf: him, smiling awkwardly with a few coworkers. You might recognize one from the incident reports.
    Everything feels frozen, like the world moved on but this room stayed waiting. A sense of order clashing with unease. He had time to put things away. He just never came back.
    And maybe he wasn’t supposed to.
