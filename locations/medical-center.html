    Medical Center

    The moment you step inside, the noise and filth of Night City drop away like a bad dream. The center's polished glass doors glide shut behind you with a soft hiss, sealing out the smog. Cool, sterile air hums through discreet vents, scented faintly with eucalyptus and something synthetic you can’t quite place—expensive, calming, and unmistakably clean. The reception area gleams under bright but indirect lighting, all clean whites and brushed steel. An artificial cherry blossom tree pulses softly with embedded biolights in the corner, and a small waterfall burbles near a couch that looks too soft to be real. The staff wear spotless white uniforms accented with corporate teal, their smiles professional but practiced—welcoming, but you still get the sense they’d call security the moment you stopped being worth the effort. Security is discreet but ever-present. Two guards stand near the entrance in fitted trauma gear, polished smartguns holstered but ready. Cameras track every movement from the ceiling, subtle but obvious enough to discourage trouble. At least one medtech is clearly ex-military by the way he watches the room rather than the monitors.
