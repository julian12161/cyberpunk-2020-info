    Precinct 12

    Main Map Zones

    1. Basement:

      • Storage: Dimly lit, dusty shelves crammed with crates of outdated uniforms, surplus gear, and broken drones. Smells faintly of mildew.
      • Armoury & Guard Room: Two reinforced doors. Inside: racks of rifles, smartguns, and riot shields under heavy maglock security. Guard room has two officers on rotation playing cards or scanning cams.
      • Evidence Locker: Sealed with biometric locks. Rows of labeled shelves and cages containing everything from cheap pistols to cyberware pieces and narcotics. An old camera blinks lazily in the corner.

    2. Ground Floor:

      • Surveillance Room: Bank of holo-monitors covering street cams, bodycams, and inside feeds. Operators rarely look up unless alarms ping.
      • Conference Room: Glass-walled, with a long table, smartboard covered in half-erased diagrams, and cheap coffee machine.
      • Locker Rooms: Metal lockers with peeling stickers. The air is thick with deodorant, sweat, and gun oil.
      • Bullpen: Rows of desks cluttered with files, braindance recorders, and cold noodles. Phones buzz constantly. The atmosphere is barely controlled chaos.

    3. 1st Floor:

      • Detective Room: Quieter but more hostile. Detectives lean over cluttered desks, slamming phones, chain-smoking, glaring at anyone out of place.
      • Toilets: Graffiti scratched into the stalls; plumbing barely works.
      • Printers/Records: Endless stacks of paper files nobody wants to digitize.
      • Briefing Room: Rows of chairs facing a podium and wall monitor. Used for roll calls and chewing-out sessions.
      • IT: Rows of terminals, junior techies half-asleep under holo-screens. Netrunner security software on constant alert.
      • Common Room: Sofa with broken springs, vending machines, big trid screen stuck on reruns. Feels like home for cops working 16-hour shifts.
      • Holding Cell (Short-Term): Chain-link cages with benches. Drunks, gangers, and unlucky scavs packed shoulder to shoulder. Smell of sweat and piss hangs heavy.

    4. 2nd Floor:

      • Interrogation Room: Bright light, one-way mirror, steel table. Chairs bolted to the floor. Interrogators swap in and out.
      • Toilets: Marginally cleaner than the first floor, reserved for detectives.
      • Chief's Office: Large wood desk, holo-plaque reading “Serve and Protect”. Expensive bourbon bottle behind the chair. Reinforced door.
      • Homicide Detectives: A smaller bullpen, quieter, walls plastered with murder boards and crime scene holos.
      • Holding Cell (Long-Term): More secure cells with reinforced doors, individual bunk and sink. Used for those awaiting transfer.
      • Roof Access: Steel hatch leads up. HVAC units hum. Sometimes snipers or patrol drones are stationed here during alerts.
      • Chief’s Storage Room: Locked side office — contains sensitive files, old case archives, and maybe a few things the Chief doesn’t want anyone to see.
