    Crystal's apartment

    The door creaks open into darkness. The air inside is stale and sour, heavy with the scent of cheap perfume, burnt wiring, and old smoke. Somewhere in the walls, you can hear rats skittering, claws tapping like rain behind thin drywall.
    The apartment is small and suffocating, its furniture sparse and worn. A massive TV flickers silently in the living room, stuck on a loop of static-laced braindance ads—its glow casting warped shadows across empty bottles and crumpled clothes.
    The only other light comes from the back room, where an old terminal hums dimly, its green glow pulsing like a dying heartbeat. Dust coats the keyboard. Something sticky stains the desk beside it. The bed’s unmade, still indented where someone last lay.
    No signs of struggle. No signs of safety either. Just silence.
