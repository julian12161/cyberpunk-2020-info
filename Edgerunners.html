    Cop

    The streets are meaner than ever, filled with new drugs, gangs, and weapons that make an M-16 look like a toy. As a city cop, you're heavily armed and armored, but often still outgunned by cyber-enhanced gangs and corporate mercenaries. Your patrols take you through the toughest neighborhoods, where each night is a firefight waiting to happen. If you're a Corporate Cop, you have access to heavy weapons, combat armor, and high-tech backup, but only patrol the clean, safe sectors. The most dangerous detail is the PsychoSquad, hunting down cyberpsychos with monstrous guns and advanced gear, but that job drives many detectives to the edge of sanity.

    Skill: Authority

    Effect: Authority represents your ability to intimidate or control others through your position as a lawman. This skill allows you to call on the forces of the Law and Government to get what you want. It includes the ability to question suspects, make arrests, and defend innocents. Authority is only effective if you appear confident; hesitation can lead to failure. The higher your Authority, the more effectively you can face down criminals, particularly high-level mobsters and officials. Authority is applied to your Cool stat.

    Bonuses:

    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to one-third (round down) of the Authority level added to the following skills:
      • Intimidate
      • Streetwise
      • Interrogation
      • Persuasion & Fast Talk
    • Initiative: Gain a bonus to Initiative equal to half the Authority level due to combat training.
    • Informants: Gain informants similar to a Fixer, but with half the points for contact generation.

      • See Also

      DN
    • Equipment & Personnel Acquisition: Use Authority to acquire equipment and personnel as needed. The cash value and difficulty of acquisition are as follows:
      • Easy (10): 1-5000eb
      • Average (15): 5001-25,000eb
      • Difficult (20): 25,001-100,000eb
      • Very Difficult (25): 100,001-500,000eb
      • Nearly Impossible (30): 500,001+eb

      The Cop can add their Leadership or Fast Talk + Persuasion skills to the roll. Availability of equipment is restricted:

      • Excellent and Common: Always available
      • Poor: Requires a Difficult (20) test
      • Rare: Requires a Nearly Impossible (30) test

      The number of personnel you can call for is based on your Authority level squared.

    Corporate

    In the Cyberpunk world, Corporations rule everything—governments, markets, nations, and armies. As a junior executive, you're on the fast track, climbing the corporate ladder amidst a cutthroat environment filled with sabotage, bribery, and blackmail. Your survival depends on navigating corporate politics and securing your position while managing high-stakes projects that range from productivity schedules to black ops missions. The pressure is immense, and ethics are often compromised. Every decision you make could be the difference between rising to power or being eliminated.

    Skill: Resources

    Effect: Resources represent your ability to command corporate assets and persuade the company to provide what you need. This includes bodyguards, weapons, vehicles, buildings, money, and more. The higher your Resources, the more you can requisition without overreaching. Resources apply to your INT stat.

    Bonuses:

    • Starting Assets: Begin with an apartment/residence, car, clothes, and other equipment, totaling the value of Resource ability squared times 500 eb. This benefit is only available while employed by the corporation.
    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to one-third (round down) of the Resource ability added to the following skills:
      • Social
      • Style
      • Persuasion & Fast Talk
    • Contacts: Gain contacts similar to a Fixer, but with half the points for contact generation.

      • See Also

      DN
    • Equipment & Personnel Acquisition: Use Resources to acquire equipment and personnel. The cash value and difficulty of acquisition are as follows:
      • Easy (10): 1-5000eb
      • Average (15): 5001-25,000eb
      • Difficult (20): 25,001-100,000eb
      • Very Difficult (25): 100,001-500,000eb
      • Nearly Impossible (30): 500,001+eb

      The Corp can add their Leadership or Fast Talk + Persuasion skills (without the Resource bonus) to the roll. Availability of equipment is restricted:

      • Excellent and Common: Always available
      • Poor: Requires a Difficult (20) test
      • Rare: Requires a Nearly Impossible (30) test

      The number of personnel you can call upon is based on your Resource level squared.

    Fixer

    You always knew you weren't cut out for the Corporate world or the life of a Solo. But you had a knack for getting people what they wanted—for a price. From small-time hustler, you've moved into the big leagues, dealing in illegal weapons, medical supplies, and high-stakes negotiations. You're part Robin Hood, part Al Capone, using a web of contacts and allies to navigate the fragmented, deadly world of the 2020s. You thrive on the Street, trading favors and information, and you're indispensable to those who need something they can't get through legitimate means. In the Cyberpunk world, they call you a Fixer.

    Skill: Streetdeal

    Effect: You always knew you weren't cut out for the Corporate world or the life of a Solo. But you had a knack for getting people what they wanted—for a price. From small-time hustler, you've moved into the big leagues, dealing in illegal weapons, medical supplies, and high-stakes negotiations. You're part Robin Hood, part Al Capone, using a web of contacts and allies to navigate the fragmented, deadly world of the 2020s. You thrive on the Street, trading favors and information, and you're indispensable to those who need something they can't get through legitimate means. In the Cyberpunk world, they call you a Fixer.

    Bonuses:

    • Specialization Bonus: Add half your Streetdeal level (rounded down) to your specialization.
    • Network Points: You receive Network points equal to your Streetdeal level squared. These points can be used to buy rerolls in your fields of expertise:

      • 2 extra rolls = 8 points
      • 3 extra rolls = 16 points
      • 4 extra rolls = 32 points
      • 5 extra rolls = 64 points
      • Unused points, especially odd ones, cannot be spent on extra rolls and are wasted.

    Media

    As a Media, you're the last line of defense against corporate corruption and government complicity. Corporations rule the world, dumping toxics, destabilizing economies, and committing murders with impunity. Governments are owned by these corporations, leaving the Media as the only force capable of exposing their crimes. Armed with a videocam and press pass, you broadcast the truth to millions, making you a national figure with fans, contracts, and corporate backing. Despite threats, pressures, and assassination attempts, you relentlessly pursue the truth, exposing scandals and injustices to the world.

    Skill: Credibility

    Effect: Determines the Media's ability to be believed by viewers, authorities, and sources. High Credibility is crucial for getting stories heard, convincing people to provide information, and gaining access to critical locations.

    • Level +3: Convince most people of minor scandals.
    • Level +5-6: Convince local officials of major issues like military atrocities
    • Level +9: Expose scandals of Watergate proportions or convince high-level authorities of extraordinary claims.

    Bonuses:

    • Reputation: Media starts with a Rep equal to their Credibility level.
    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to one-third of the Credibility level added to the following skills:
      • Fast Talk/Persuasion
      • Interview
      • Oratory
      • Library Search
      • Streetwise
    • Contacts: Media gains contacts similar to a Fixer

      • See Also

      DN
    • Corporate Resources: If working for a corporation, Credibility can be used as a Resource skill, providing perks as long as the Media remains with the corporation.

    Medtechie

    In a world where medicine intersects with mechanics, Medtechies are invaluable. They can perform black market surgeries faster than most people can fix a toaster, making them indispensable to Solos needing wounds patched or illegal cybernetics installed. However, this clandestine work also creates enemies. A new job with Trauma Team Inc.™ might offer some stability, but the dangers remain.

    Skill: MedTech

    Effect: Used to perform major surgery and medical repairs. For more detailed descriptions, refer to Trauma Team, pg. 116.

    Bonuses:

    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to one-third of the MedTech level added to the following skills:
      • Human Perception
      • Diagnose Illness
      • Pharmaceutical
      • Library Search gains a bonus equal to the MedTech level, but only when researching medical information.
    • Medical Supplies Discount: Licensed Medtechies (requiring a SIN) can acquire medical supplies, pharmaceuticals, and surgery equipment at discounted rates, as detailed in Wildside, pg. 52. Cyberware can also be bought cheaper, provided it is not illegal or black market.
    • Corporate Perks: If employed by a corporation, a Medtechie can use their MedTech ability as a Resource skill, granting access to perks such as cars, apartments, and other non-combat, non-high-tech benefits.

    Netrunner

    From a young age, Netrunners have been prodigies in the digital world. By fifth grade, they were already hacking school mainframes, and by thirteen, they had financed their first neural interface plugs through illicit means. With a direct mental link to computers, Netrunners dive into the Net, the worldwide telecommunications system. They are elite hackers, seamlessly slipping into the toughest mainframe systems. Their defense and offense programs are ready at the mental touch, capable of toppling data fortresses from major corporations like EBM, ITI, and SonyMatsushita-Ford. While uncovering corporate treachery or deadly secrets is part of the job, Netrunners live for the thrill of the next big hack. They know the risks—advanced counter-intrusion programs and smart AIs—but they thrive on the edge, headfirst in the Net.

    Skill: Interface

    Effect: Reflects the Netrunner's ability to manipulate Interface programs. This skill is used for various Menu functions such as locating Remotes, running software, controlling Remotes, downlinking, loading, creating, and deleting programs. Interface is based on the INT Stat.

    Bonuses:

    • Programming Hours: Netrunners get hours for program creation calculated by doubling their Interface skill level and then squaring it.
    • Program Difficulty: The difficulty level of any program created cannot exceed the combined total of the Netrunner’s INT, LUCK, and Programming skills.

    Nomad

    Ten years ago, corporations drove your family off the farm, and you joined a growing group of homeless families to form a Nomad pack. Now, your pack—comprised of nearly two hundred members—roams the freeways in a fleet of cars, vans, buses, and RVs. This mobile town on wheels has teachers, MedTechs, leaders, and mechanics, all related by marriage or kinship. You seek supplies, odd jobs, and spare parts in a fragmented society. Sometimes you trade labor for food or cash, while less lawful packs hire out as muscle in corporate wars. Your vehicles are armored and armed, every kid knows how to use a rifle, and everyone packs a knife. Life as a Nomad is tough, but you thrive in this world.

    The most visible Nomads are Scouts—leather-armored riders on bikes or fast muscle cars who protect the convoy and find safe campsites. As a Scout, you navigate through trouble, whether it's rival packs, law enforcement, or corporate enforcers. Like a modern-day cowboy, you ride the hard trail with your gun and bike, embodying the Nomad spirit.

    Skill: Family

    Effect: Allows Nomads to call upon the resources and help of their extended tribal family, including weapons, cash, information, or even a small army. The higher your Family ability, the more important you are within the pack and the more support you can summon.

    • Level +2: Several pack members to assist with tasks like wrecking a town.
    • Level +7-8: Major decision-making and troop leadership.
    • Level +10: Potentially the leader of the pack.

    Bonuses:

    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to one-third of the Family ability added to the following skills:
      • Social
      • Leadership

    Rockerboys

    Rockerboys are the street poets, rebels, and social consciences of the 2000s. Armed with digital porta-studios and garage laser disk mastering, they spread their messages far and wide, from record stores to comsats. Often, their messages challenge the powers that be—Corporations and Governments alike. With roots tracing back to legends like Dylan, Springsteen, and The Who, Rockerboys use their music to inspire, inform, and lead. Whether through protest songs or electrifying rock n' roll, they rally people to action, exposing corruption and toppling regimes. A Rockerboy’s power lies in their ability to galvanize the masses, transforming gigs into movements.

    Skill: Charismatic Leadership

    Effect: Allows the Rockerboy to sway crowds. The number of people influenced equals the Rocker's level squared times 200, added to their Cool stat.

    Usage: This skill can incite crowds to take significant actions, such as rioting or attacking fortified positions. The higher the Charismatic Leadership level, the larger and more complex the actions a crowd can be persuaded to undertake.

    • Level +3: Incite a nightclub crowd to become rowdy.
    • Level +5-6: Provoke a concert crowd of thousands to trash a nearby neighbourhood.
    • Level +9: Possess a mesmeric ability akin to historical figures like Adolf Hitler, capable of raising armies, starting movements, and destroying nations.

    Bonuses:

    • Performance Skill: Gain a bonus equal to half of Charismatic Leadership (round down).
    • Social and Seduction Skills: Gain a bonus equal to a third of Charismatic Leadership.
    • Musical Equipment: Access to musical equipment (including cyber implants for vocals) valued at half Charismatic Leadership times 500 eb.

    Solo

    Solos are elite fighting machines of the Cyberpunk world, whether working as freelance mercenaries or Corporate cybersoldiers. Most Solos have military backgrounds, serving in Corporate armies or Government "police actions." Over time, they rely more on cyberware—cyberlimbs for weapons and armor, bio-program chips for enhanced reflexes and awareness, and combat drugs for an edge in battle. The best Solos might become ronin, offering their lethal skills to the highest bidder. However, this life comes with a heavy price: a body more machine than flesh, killing reflexes that are hard to control, severe addictions, and a pervasive distrust of others. The price is steep, but for a Solo, it’s worth it.

    Skill: Combat Sense

    Effect: Enhances the Solo's ability to perceive danger, notice traps, and avoid harm. Combat Sense provides a bonus to both Awareness skill and Initiative equal to the Solo's level in Combat Sense.

    Bonuses:

    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to half of the Combat Sense level added to the following skills:
      • Awareness
      • Notice
    • Combat Specialization: Choose one combat skill to receive a bonus equal to a third of Combat Sense. Options include:
      • Light, Medium, Heavy, Very Heavy Handgun
      • Light, Medium, Heavy SMG
      • Sniper Rifle, Assault Rifle, Shotgun
      • Bow, Crossbow, Brawling
      • Sword, Knife, Martial Arts, Club, Improvised Weapon
      • Specific Heavy Weapon (MGs, Grenades, etc.)
    • Cyberware: Access to 500 eb worth of cyberware per point of Combat Sense. This cyberware is installed without surgery costs, but no compensation is provided for replaced body parts.

    Techie

    Techies are the tinkerers and fixers of the Cyberpunk world, always ready to disassemble and reassemble anything that crosses their path. With screwdrivers and wrenches always at hand, they can solve any technical problem—whether it's a downed computer, a malfunctioning hydrogen burner, or a glitchy interface plug. Their skills are essential in a world where few understand how technology truly works. While fixing everyday items pays the bills, real money comes from big jobs: illegal weapons, stolen cybertech, corporate espionage gear, and gadgets like thermite bombs and hunter-killer robots. Successful Techies reinvest their earnings into new gadgets and hardware, always looking for innovative applications. However, their black market work also earns them enemies, necessitating strong defense systems and occasionally calling in favors from Solos.

    Skill: Jury Rig

    Effect: Allows the Techie to temporarily repair or alter anything for 1D6 turns per level squared. This repair is not permanent and will break down after the elapsed time.

    Bonuses:

    • Skill Enhancements: Gain a bonus equal to one-third of the Jury Rig level added to the following skills:
      • Aero Tech
      • AV Tech
      • Basic Tech
      • Cyber Tech
      • Electronics
      • Gyro Tech
      • Weapon Tech
    • Tech Tools: Access to tech tools valued at 500 eb per level of Jury Rig.
    • Repair Costs: Can buy broken goods and repair them for 3/4 price or less, with the cost determined by the complexity and assigned difficulty number by the referee.
